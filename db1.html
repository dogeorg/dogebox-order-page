NanoPC-T6 LTS (as "T6") is a high-performance open-source platform for edge computing, designed and developed by the FriendlyElec team.

It has two 2.5G Ethernet ports and is based on Rockchip's RK3588. It features 4GB/8GB/16GB LPDDR4x RAM and an optional 32GB/64GB/256GB eMMC flash. Compatible with systems including OpenMediaVault, FriendlyWrt, Android, Android TV, Debian, Ubuntu, etc. Supports GPU and VPU acceleration.

T6 is a compact board of 110 x 80 mm with rich hardware resources and an optional CNC metal case. It has 2 HDMI output ports and 1 HDMI IN port. T6 can play video streams including 8K60p H.265/VP9, 8K30p H264, etc., and record video streams including 4K60p H.265. It has one M.2 B-Key slot for NVME SSD disks and one M.2 E-Key for a 2230 WiFi module.

Additionally, T6 includes one USB 3.0 port, two USB 2.0 ports, and one full-featured USB-C port powered by DC-12V.

In summary, T6 is well-suited for enterprise customers developing mini machine vision systems with multiple Ethernet ports and for embedded system hobbyists exploring and implementing prototype designs.
